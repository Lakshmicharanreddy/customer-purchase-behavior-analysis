# customer-purchase-behavior-analysis
##Project Overview

This project analyzes customer purchase behavior using a real-world e-commerce dataset. The objective of the analysis is to identify patterns in customer activity, sales trends, product performance, and seller contribution in order to generate business insights that can support data-driven decision making.

The analysis combines SQL-based data exploration with Python-based data visualization to better understand the key factors influencing revenue and customer satisfaction.

##Dataset

Dataset used: Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset by Olist

The dataset contains multiple relational tables representing an online marketplace.

##Main tables used in the analysis include:

 customers

 orders

order_items

order_payments

products

product_category

sellers

order_reviews

geolocation data

##Key Insights

  Customer purchasing behavior shows that a large portion of users place only one order, highlighting an opportunity to improve customer retention strategies.
  
  Sales activity fluctuates over time with noticeable peaks during certain periods, indicating potential seasonal demand patterns.
  
  Product category analysis reveals that a limited number of categories contribute significantly to overall sales.
  
  Seller performance analysis shows that a small group of sellers generates a large share of marketplace revenue.
  
  Customer reviews indicate generally positive satisfaction levels, though delivery delays tend to result in lower ratings.

##Business Recommendations

  Improve customer retention through loyalty programs and personalized promotions.
  
  Focus marketing and inventory strategies on high-performing product categories.
  
  Strengthen relationships with top-performing sellers while supporting smaller sellers.
  
  Improve logistics operations to reduce delivery delays and maintain high customer satisfaction.
